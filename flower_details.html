Tulip

A vibrant flower perfect for sunny gardens, symbolizing love and renewal.

“Flowers heal me. Tulips make me happy. I surround myself with them when they arrive from the fields in La Conner, not far from where I live.”
– Rebecca Wells

Basic Information

Scientific Name

Tulipa

Common Name

Tulip

Flower Family

Liliaceae

Color Options

Red, Yellow, Pink, Purple, White

Care Guide

  • Sunlight: Full Sun
  • Watering: Moderate, well-drained soil
  • Cold Tolerance: Can handle spring frost
  • Pruning: Remove wilted flowers to promote regrowth

Safety Information

Warning: Tulips contain toxic compounds that are harmful to pets and humans. Ingestion can cause vomiting, diarrhea, and skin irritation. Handle with care and keep out of reach of children and animals.

Description

Tulips thrive in full sunlight and well-drained soil, making them ideal for spring gardens.

Meaning & Symbolism

Tulips symbolize perfect love, renewal, and springtime beauty.
